Class system

How the class system works

There are 6 classes of vehicles, notated using the letters A-F, with A being the simpler vehicles and F being more complex. There are also 3 sub-classes: p ("passenger". Vehicles for carrying a large number of passengers ), s ("shipping". Vehicles for carrying large amounts of cargo), and m ("military". Vehicles with weapons).

Licenses to drive are marked using the same letters. A higher license allows operation of lower vehicles as well. For example, a Bp license allows the piloting of class A, B, and C vehicles, whether or not they carry a large number of passengers.

Description of the classes

  1. Surface vehicles. Cannot leave the surface of a planet/moon. Cars and boats are in this class.
  2. Aerial vehicles. Can leave the ground, but not the atmosphere. Planes are in this class.
  3. Low orbit vehicles. Can leave the atmosphere, but can't go much further. Satellites and space-ports are in this class, along with the vehicles designed to get to them from the surface.
  4. High orbit vehicles. Capable of travling between a planet and its moons, but not between different planets.
  5. Inter-planetary vehicles. Capable of travling between planets within the same star system.
  6. Inter-stellar vehicles. Capable of traveling between different star systems.
